## Detailed Scoring Matrix

### Scoring Methodology

**Time to First Dollar (T2FD):** How long until the idea generates revenue?
- 1 week: Immediate revenue possible (merch)
- 2 weeks: Revenue by week 2-3 (content)
- 4+ weeks: Revenue deferred (complex products)

**Month 1 Revenue Potential:** Realistic income from idea in first 30 days
- $500+: High-probability revenue (merch, YouTube)
- $100-500: Likely revenue (horror content)
- $0-100: Validation phase only (agents, Android)

**Implementation Effort:** Hours required in Month 1
- <20 hrs: Quick (setup + launch)
- 20-35 hrs: Standard (build + test)
- >35 hrs: Heavy (complex development)

**ROI Score:** Revenue potential ÷ Effort investment (hours)
- ROI = (Expected revenue / Effort) × 100
- High ROI (>$10/hr): Should prioritize
- Medium ROI ($5-10/hr): Viable, worth doing
- Low ROI (<$5/hr): Deferred or parallel-track

**Break-even Timeline:** When does cumulative revenue exceed time investment cost?
- Assume $50/hr as "opportunity cost" of Moises's time
- E.g., 30 hours × $50 = $1500 investment
- Need $1500+ revenue to break even

## Strategic Sequencing: Parallel vs Sequential

### Month 1: Parallel Execution (All 4 launches)
**Why:** Ideas don't block each other; Moises can context-switch daily. Risk of spreading thin, but upside of parallel validation.

**Daily schedule (suggested):**
- Morning (2h): Merch (designs, Etsy setup)
- Midday (2h): Agents (spec, coding)
- Afternoon (2h): Horror/YouTube (scripting, production)
- Evening (2h): Async tasks (research, admin, planning)

### Month 2: Conditional Sequencing
Based on Month 1 results:
- **If 2+ ideas strong:** Keep them in parallel, reduce others
- **If 1 clear winner:** Allocate 60 hrs/week to winner, 10 hrs/week to #2 idea
- **If all weak:** Pivot one idea (e.g., #12 Android → different product)

### Month 3: Specialization
- **Likely state:** 1-2 ideas at scale, others killed or in maintenance mode
- **Reallocation:** Move 30-40 hours from weaklings to winners
- **New project:** If winners demand it, hire contractor or introduce new idea

## Appendix: Decision Framework for Idea Prioritization

### Questions to Ask for Any New Idea
1. **Time-to-First-Dollar:** How many weeks until revenue?
2. **Effort required:** How many hours in Month 1?
3. **Market size:** Is there proven demand?
4. **Defensibility:** Do you have an advantage?
5. **Synergies:** Does it help other ideas?
6. **Your expertise:** Can you execute well?
7. **Capital required:** How much upfront cost?
8. **Opportunity cost:** What would you NOT do if you did this?

### Kill Decision Triggers
Execute these checks at Month 1 and Month 3 boundaries:
- [ ] Are revenues ≥10% of effort investment cost ($1500 for 30 hrs)?
- [ ] Is user engagement positive (NPS >5, retention >50%)?
- [ ] Can you see a path to $1K/mo within 6 months?
- [ ] Does the founder still believe in the idea?

If all four are "No," kill it. Redeploy resources to winning ideas.

## Final Word

**The goal of Month 1 is validation, not perfection.**

Execute all 4 launches in parallel. Measure ruthlessly. By end of Month 1, you'll have data on:
- Which customers exist (and how many)
- What they're willing to pay
- How hard is it to serve them
- Whether you enjoy the work

That data will be worth far more than perfect execution of a "sure winner"—because most startups fail on market fit, not execution.

**Month 2:** Double down on 2-3 winners. Kill 1-2 losers without guilt.  
**Month 3:** Optimize what works. Launch new bets from lessons learned.  
**Year 1:** If any idea hits $10K/mo, hire help. If all stay <$5K/mo, reconsider focus.
