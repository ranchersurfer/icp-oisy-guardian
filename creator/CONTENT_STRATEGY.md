## Three Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Horror Entertainment (40% of output)
**Platforms:** TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels  
**Audience:** Gen Z / Gen X horror fans, gaming community  
**Cadence:** 2–3 posts/week  
**Formats:**
- Short horror stories (30–90 seconds)
- Movie/game reviews with spooky clips
- "Horror facts" (psychological elements, true stories)
- Scary game clips with reactions
- Haunted locations / paranormal deep dives

**Examples:**
- "What horror movie villain represents your MBTI type?"
- "This game has the scariest AI enemy I've ever seen"
- "The most disturbing detail from [horror film] you probably missed"

**Tone:** Spooky, entertaining, not gratuitous. Respect the genre.

### Pillar 2: AI & Automation Tutorials (35% of output)
**Platforms:** YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn  
**Audience:** Small business owners, ops managers, curious tech folks  
**Cadence:** 2–3 posts/week  
**Formats:**
- How-to: "Automate X without code" (5-min tutorials)
- Case study: "We saved [Company] 20 hours/week with OpenClaw"
- FAQ: "Top questions about AI automation answered"
- Myths: "AI automation won't replace your job" (honest take)
- Behind-the-scenes: Show OpenClaw architecture, agent design

**Examples:**
- "Here's how to automate email triage in 10 minutes"
- "Why Haiku models > expensive AI for business automation"
- "Mistake #1: Using AI for everything. Here's what actually works."

**Tone:** Educational, practical, no fluff. Honest about limitations.

### Pillar 3: OpenClaw & Developer Tips (25% of output)
**Platforms:** Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Discord (self-promo)  
**Audience:** Developers, DevOps, automation builders  
**Cadence:** 1–2 posts/week  
**Formats:**
- Tips: "How to structure memory in OpenClaw agents" (threading)
- Benchmarks: "Haiku vs Sonnet cost comparison with real data"
- Snippets: Useful code or config patterns
- Updates: Feature releases, roadmap info
- Wins: Celebrate community wins with OpenClaw

**Examples:**
- "Protip: Use temporal decay in memory search for fresher context"
- "Built a price-monitoring agent in 30 lines of code. Here's how."
- "OpenClaw cost optimization: 70-80% reduction is possible. Here's our setup."

**Tone:** Insider knowledge, helpful, community-first.

## Tone & Style Guide

**Do:**
- Be authentic (share real opinions, not corporate speak)
- Educate first, entertain second
- Ask questions (engage your audience)
- Credit inspiration / data sources
- Use strong visuals (match mood to content)
- Test different formats, track what works

**Don't:**
- Spread misinformation (fact-check scary claims)
- Be overly promotional (we're here to help, not sell)
- Copy competitors directly (remix, don't replicate)
- Ignore comments (respond, build community)
- Post low-effort content (every post matters)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### TikTok & IG Reels
- Hook first 3 seconds (scroll-stop)
- Vertical video (9:16)
- Text overlay (captions on + audio)
- End screen call-to-action (link in bio, follow, DM)

### YouTube Shorts
- Same as TikTok, but optimized for YouTube algo
- Custom thumbnails with text/faces
- Playlists: Group by series (horror lore, tutorial series)

### LinkedIn
- Professional but approachable tone
- Data/insights lead posts (not just links)
- Tag relevant communities
- Call-to-action: "What's your biggest automation challenge?"

### Twitter/X
- Threads: Multi-tweet deep-dives
- Threads Quotes on trending topics
- Engage with other tech/horror accounts (community building)
